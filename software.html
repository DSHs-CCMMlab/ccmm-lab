Tools We Use

Software Suite

All major computational packages actively used by CCMM Lab members in their research.

⚛️
Quantum Chemistry
Gaussian 16
Revision C.01
The industry-standard quantum chemistry package for electronic structure calculations. Used in the lab for DFT geometry optimizations, frequency calculations, transition state searches, NBO analysis, and energy decomposition on metal–oxo complexes and organic reaction mechanisms.
Used for: DFT, IRC, NBO, TD-DFT, Thermochemistry
→ gaussian.com
🔬
Quantum Chemistry
ORCA
Version 5.x
A highly versatile and efficient quantum chemistry package particularly strong for open-shell transition metal systems. The lab uses ORCA for high-accuracy single-point energies, coupled cluster calculations (DLPNO-CCSD(T)), multireference calculations, and spectroscopic property prediction on iron–oxo and related complexes.
Used for: DLPNO-CCSD(T), CASSCF, EPR/NMR spectra, spin densities
→ orcaforum.kofo.mpg.de
🧮
Quantum Chemistry
Turbomole
Version 7.x
A highly efficient quantum chemistry program optimized for speed on medium to large molecular systems. In the lab it is used for DFT calculations with resolution-of-identity (RI) approximations, excited-state calculations using TDDFT, and geometry optimizations where computational efficiency is critical.
Used for: RI-DFT, TDDFT, large system QM calculations
→ turbomole.org
🌊
Molecular Dynamics
AMBER 24
AmberTools24 + Amber24
The premier MD package for biomolecular simulations. The lab uses AMBER for protein–ligand MD simulations, free energy perturbation (FEP) calculations, MM-GBSA/PBSA binding free energy estimates, and setting up QM/MM calculations of enzyme active sites. GPU acceleration enables microsecond-scale simulations of large systems.
Used for: Protein MD, MM-GBSA, FEP, system preparation for QM/MM
→ ambermd.org
⚡
Molecular Dynamics
GROMACS
Version 2024.x
One of the fastest and most widely used open-source MD engines, excellent for both proteins and small molecules. In the lab, GROMACS is used for protein dynamics, ligand binding free energy calculations, enhanced sampling simulations (metadynamics, umbrella sampling), and large-scale comparative MD studies.
Used for: MD, Metadynamics, Umbrella Sampling, PMF calculations
→ gromacs.org
🧬
Molecular Dynamics
NAMD
Version 3.x
A high-performance parallel MD program developed for large biomolecular systems, particularly suited for membrane proteins and nucleic acids. NAMD is used in the lab alongside VMD for visualization of long MD trajectories, steered MD pulling simulations, and adaptive sampling methods.
Used for: Large biomolecular systems, steered MD, membrane simulations
→ ks.uiuc.edu/namd
🎯
Molecular Docking
AutoDock-GPU
GPU-Accelerated Edition
The GPU-accelerated version of the AutoDock docking suite, enabling rapid virtual screening of large compound libraries against biological targets. In the CCMM Lab, AutoDock-GPU powers the first stage of the drug discovery pipeline — identifying hit molecules from databases such as ZINC and PubChem — with throughput orders of magnitude faster than CPU-only runs.
Used for: Virtual screening, binding pose prediction, pharmacophore validation
→ github.com/AutoDock-GPU
🔗
QM/MM
ChemShell
Py-ChemShell 23.x
A modular QM/MM framework that seamlessly interfaces quantum chemistry packages (ORCA, Gaussian) with molecular mechanics engines (AMBER, DL_POLY). The lab uses ChemShell for hybrid QM/MM simulations of enzyme active sites, enabling quantum-level accuracy on the catalytic core while treating the surrounding protein environment classically at a fraction of the computational cost.
Used for: Enzyme QM/MM, active site reactivity, QM/MM geometry optimization
→ chemshell.org
