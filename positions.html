Open

PhD Scholar (JRF / SRF)

We invite applications from highly motivated candidates for doctoral research in computational chemistry. Projects are available in (1) DFT studies of metal–oxo complexes and C–H activation, (2) Machine Learning for chemical property prediction, and (3) integrated MD/Docking/QM–MM pipeline for drug discovery and enzyme catalysis. Funding is available through national fellowship schemes (CSIR/UGC/DST INSPIRE) or institute teaching assistantships.

Requirements
  • M.Sc. in Chemistry, Physics, Biochemistry, or a related field
  • Qualified in CSIR-NET / UGC-NET / GATE or equivalent national exam
  • Interest in computational methods (prior experience is a plus, not mandatory)
  • Basic programming skills (Python, shell scripting) are advantageous

Project / M.Sc. Dissertation Student

Short-term project positions are available for M.Sc. students who wish to carry out their dissertation research in the CCMM Lab. Students will gain hands-on experience with industry-standard computational chemistry software including Gaussian, ORCA, GROMACS, and AutoDock. Projects are tailored to the student's background and available time frame.

Culture

Who We Are Looking For

Beyond qualifications, here is what makes someone a great fit for the CCMM Lab.

🔍

Scientific Curiosity

A genuine desire to understand how and why reactions happen at the atomic level — not just to get the right answer, but to understand what it means.

💻

Computational Mindset

Comfort with working on Linux systems, running command-line tools, and a willingness to learn new software. Prior coding experience is a strong plus.

🤝

Collaborative Spirit

The lab's strength comes from team members supporting each other. We value open communication, knowledge sharing, and a positive attitude.

📈

Drive to Publish

We aim to produce impactful, rigorous research. Students who are motivated to see their work appear in high-quality international journals thrive here.

Ready to apply or just want to learn more?

Send your CV and a short paragraph about your research interests to Dr. Mandal. Even if there is no formal opening listed, we are always happy to hear from exceptional candidates.
